# Monetization

## Purpose

This document defines the free and paid boundaries of English OS.

The goal is to monetize intelligence, personalization, and depth without breaking the product's core trust.

## Monetization Philosophy

English OS should not depend on locking basic product value behind artificial friction.

The free tier should be useful enough for a learner to genuinely experience the operating-system value of the product:

- structure
- roadmap clarity
- curated resources
- progress visibility

The paid tier should unlock stronger personalization, deeper intelligence, and more advanced feedback loops.

## Free Tier

The free tier should include:

- access to the core product shell
- basic onboarding and learner profile setup
- personal roadmap basics
- curated resource access
- saved learning history
- basic progress tracking
- basic review and repetition support
- basic next-step recommendations

The free tier should be good enough that the product feels real, credible, and habit-forming.

## Paid Tier

The paid tier should focus on higher-value intelligence and deeper support:

- adaptive AI recommendations
- personalized roadmap updates
- AI writing feedback
- AI speaking feedback
- retention analysis
- deeper learner insights
- premium planning and review flows

Paid should feel like the product understands the learner better and helps them improve more efficiently.

## Likely Upgrade Triggers

Users are most likely to upgrade when they want:

- more precise recommendations
- better support for writing and speaking
- deeper understanding of weaknesses
- better retention and review intelligence
- a more personalized path than the baseline free system gives them

## Monetization Boundary Rules

These rules should guide future pricing decisions:

1. Do not paywall the entire system identity.
The learner should still feel the roadmap and control-center value on free.

2. Paywall intelligence, not basic dignity.
Core tracking, clarity, and coherent use should not feel intentionally crippled.

3. Paid should feel like leverage, not punishment.
The upgrade should save time, improve confidence, and sharpen guidance.

4. Do not turn the product into a content subscription library.
The value should remain rooted in system quality, not content hoarding.

## V1 Packaging Direction

The most likely V1 packaging is:

- free for roadmap, curated resources, progress basics, and saved learning history
- paid for deeper personalization, AI feedback, advanced review intelligence, and richer planning flows
