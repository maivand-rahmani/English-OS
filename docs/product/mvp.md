# MVP Definition

## Purpose

This document defines the first real launchable version of English OS.

It translates the product thesis into a concrete V1 that can be built, shipped, and used as a real product.

## V1 Summary

English OS V1 is a system-first product for self-learners who want structure, trusted resources, and one clear place to manage English learning.

It is not an AI-first app, not a course platform, and not a content-heavy learning library.

V1 should feel real, focused, polished, and extensible rather than broad and unfinished.

## Target User For V1

The first version is for:

- beginner self-learners
- intermediate self-learners
- people learning independently without a teacher-led system
- learners who want a clear plan more than more content

The first version is not primarily optimized for:

- exam preparation
- classroom management
- enterprise language training
- children
- advanced learners with highly specialized goals

## Core Jobs To Be Done

V1 should help the learner:

- understand where they are and where they are going
- get a clear roadmap instead of studying randomly
- find trusted resources without endless searching
- know what to do today and what to do next
- track progress and learning history in one place
- review material before it is forgotten
- practice writing and speaking inside structured workspaces

## Core V1 Product Shape

V1 should combine four layers:

1. Operating system layer
Dashboard, navigation, roadmap, review, and progress visibility.

2. Planning layer
Personal roadmap and next-step guidance.

3. Curation layer
Curated resources with strong recommendation context.

4. Practice layer
Writing and speaking workspaces connected to the learner's broader system.

## Must-Have Product Capabilities

V1 must include:

- onboarding that creates an initial learner profile
- a dashboard that acts as a control center
- a personal roadmap with stages or blocks
- a curated resource system
- a unified progress history
- a review and repetition system
- writing as a real practice workspace
- speaking as a real practice workspace
- basic recommendations for what to do next

## V1 AI Position

AI in V1 should be present but not dominant.

AI should act as a lightweight assistant layer on top of the system for:

- recommendation support
- writing feedback
- speaking feedback
- insight generation

The product must still make sense, feel useful, and remain coherent even without deep AI orchestration.

## What V1 Must Prove

V1 must prove that English OS can deliver:

- clarity instead of chaos
- trusted resource navigation
- a stable daily learning workflow
- visible progress and review structure
- meaningful value beyond generic study advice

## Product Quality Bar

V1 should be:

- fast to launch without being rushed in design quality
- technically clean enough to evolve
- visually polished enough to feel like a real product
- narrow enough to stay coherent
- structured enough to support deeper AI and analytics later

## V1 Success Criteria

V1 is successful if a learner can:

- complete onboarding and understand their learning setup quickly
- open the dashboard and immediately know what to do next
- follow a roadmap without needing to search all over the internet
- trust the recommended resources
- log work and see progress in one system
- return for both guidance and practice
